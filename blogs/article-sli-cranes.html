Safe Load Indicators for Cranes:
The Engineering Behind Every Safe Lift

§01 — Introduction

Every crane lift reduces to a single number: actual load as a percentage of rated capacity. A Safe Load Indicator makes that number visible, audible, and — above 100% — physically impossible to ignore.

A Safe Load Indicator (SLI) — also called a Load Moment Indicator (LMI) or Rated Capacity Indicator (RCI) — continuously compares the actual load moment against the crane's certified rated capacity envelope. When load approaches 90%, it warns. At 100%, it stops the hoist. No bypass. No override.

In India, fitment of a certified SLI is mandatory under IS 4573 for all mobile cranes. Globally, EN 13000 (Europe), ASME B30.5 (USA), and ISO 10245 mandate SLI/LMI on all modern lifting equipment.

§02 — Before vs After: What SLI Changes on Every Site

⚠ Before SLI — At Risk
  • No load measurement — unknown load at all times
  • Guesswork and visual estimation only
  • Overload possible on any lift
  • Boom failure and structural damage risk
  • No data logging — no audit trail
  • Non-compliant with IS 4573 / EN 13000
  • Operator life at risk on every shift
  • Insurance cover void on site incidents
✓ After SLI — Protected
  • Live load display confirmed safe at rated %
  • Auto hoist inhibit relay at 100% — hardwired
  • Audible alarm from 90% pre-warning threshold
  • IS 4573 / EN 13000 / ASME B30 compliant
  • 256 MB data log — events timestamped
  • Full audit trail — USB and Bluetooth export
  • Operator fully protected on every lift
  • Insurance and site liability protected
